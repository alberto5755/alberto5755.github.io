Cybernetic Hand

Home Work

I created this model following the blocking technique to get the main shape of the model, with primitive objects (cubes and cylinders).
I then started adding details with boleans and thanks to plugins such as Hard Ops and Box Cutter, I was able to make the workflow more efficient.
Furthermore the cables where made with the help of the Cablerator, another plugin for Blender, which creates automatically a path which targets a circle in order to create the cable, I then applied a hook for each one of them, so when the filgers will move, the cables will stretch.
I also added some details in the inside of the hand to increase the realism.
Once the hand was modelled, I started to rig it, with basic bone parenting (for weight painting) and bone constraints, therefore I just need to control a single bone to animate an entire finger.
Regarding the materials, I mostly applied a metallic Principle BSDFs, with slight color variations, as I believe it corresponds the most to this model, finally in order to increase the realism of this material I added a procedural noise texture to give a slight bump to the metal.
This technique has also been used for the grip material on the palm of the hand, which was essentially made with a simple BSDF and a low roughness.
